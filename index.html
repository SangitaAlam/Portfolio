Projects

Bluetooth Controlled Car

I developed a Bluetooth-controlled car designed to record its surroundings, allowing the controller to view the environment on a monitor in real-time. The project utilized motor drivers, an Arduino, and a lithium battery to power and control the vehicle. A standout feature of this project is the rear-mounted camera that captures and streams video footage. Successfully submitted as the final project for our EDC lab course, this project showcased my ability to integrate hardware and software for practical applications in robotics.

Cricky

I developed "Cricky," a robotic arm designed to pick and place various objects with precision. The project aimed to create a versatile tool for handling different items. Utilizing an arm kit, motor drivers, a Bluetooth module, a lithium battery, and an Arduino, Cricky featured a highly functional arm capable of picking up objects. A significant challenge was ensuring the arm received sufficient power; we overcame this by increasing the battery power supply. Successfully implemented, Cricky demonstrated effective integration of robotics hardware and control systems.
